1980'S

The three main subcultures of the 90s were Grunge fashion pioneered by Marc Jacobs and Alexander McQueen, (loose-fitting, worn-out jeans, flannel shirts, Doc Martens, and chunky boots) the preppy style, (oversized shirts, high-waisted jeans, plaid mini-skirts) and Punk (black leather jackets, ripped jeans, choker necklaces). In addition, women wore overalls, swirl patterns, leopard print, scrunchies, bandanas, crop tops, tie-dye… The beginning of the 1990s was the peak of the Supermodel era.

Totally Rad: A Blast From The 80's Fashion Past

The 1980s was a decade of excess, from hairspray to leg warmers. Fashion mirrored this extravagance, with bold colors, loud patterns, and statement pieces taking center stage. Whether you craved comfort or craved attention, 80s fashion had something for everyone.

One of the defining trends of the era was the power suit. Women embraced sharp tailoring and broad shoulders, conveying a sense of confidence and ambition. This look was popularized by TV shows like Dynasty and Dallas, where working women ruled the boardroom in style. Power dressing wasn't just for work, either. Women incorporated tailored jackets and statement blazers into their everyday wardrobes.

Beyond the Boardroom: Athletic Influences and Everyday Style

The 80s also saw a rise in athletic wear, thanks in part to the aerobics craze. Leggings, leg warmers, and brightly colored workout gear transitioned from the gym to the streets. Daywear became more casual, with ripped denim, mom jeans, and neon-colored t-shirts becoming staples.

Men's fashion in the 80s was all about individuality. Punk rock and new wave music influenced everyday style, with ripped jeans, leather jackets, and graphic t-shirts becoming popular choices. Preppy styles were also in, featuring polo shirts, boat shoes, and pastel color palettes. And of course, no look was complete without a Members Only jacket.
